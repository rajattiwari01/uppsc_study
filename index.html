📅 18-Month Master Study Plan

Phase-wise preparation from now to October 2026

⚡ Key Strategy: Since you have ~18 months, divide into 4 phases. Start with dental subjects (highest weightage), parallel current affairs from Day 1, and GS in Phase 2-3. Last 3 months = revision + mock tests only.
Phase 1: May 2025 – Aug 2025 (4 months)
🏗️ Foundation Phase – Core Dental Subjects
  • Oral Anatomy & Histology – 3 weeks (Tooth morphology, development, muscles, TMJ, histology of oral tissues)
  • Dental Anatomy – 2 weeks (Tooth numbering, morphology, occlusion)
  • General Anatomy (Head & Neck) – 3 weeks (Cranial nerves, blood supply, spaces, triangles)
  • General & Dental Pharmacology – 3 weeks (LA, analgesics, antibiotics, drug interactions)
  • Oral Pathology & Microbiology – 3 weeks (Cysts, tumors, infections, developmental disorders)
  • General Pathology – 2 weeks (Inflammation, neoplasia, immunology basics)
📌 Parallel Task: Start reading daily news (The Hindu/Indian Express) 30 mins/day. Note down health/dental/UP-related news. Solve 10 PYQs daily from any completed topic.
Phase 2: Sep 2025 – Jan 2026 (5 months)
📚 Advanced Phase – Clinical Subjects + GS Start
  • Oral Medicine & Radiology – 3 weeks (Systemic diseases, oral manifestations, radiographic interpretations)
  • Conservative Dentistry & Endodontics – 3 weeks (Cavity preparation, RCT, materials, instruments)
  • Prosthodontics – 3 weeks (Complete/partial dentures, implants, maxillofacial prosthetics)
  • Oral & Maxillofacial Surgery – 3 weeks (Extractions, fractures, infections, tumors, LA techniques)
  • Periodontics – 2 weeks (Classification, treatment, instruments, recent advances)
  • Orthodontics – 2 weeks (Cephalometrics, classification, appliances, growth)
  • Pedodontics – 2 weeks (Behavior management, pulp therapy, preventive dentistry)
  • Dental Materials – 2 weeks (Properties, types, manipulation, recent advances)
  • General Studies START – Indian Polity, History basics, Geography of India & UP
📌 Parallel Task: Continue daily CA (now 45 mins). Start solving subject-wise PYQs after completing each subject. Weekly revision of CA notes.
Phase 3: Feb 2026 – Jun 2026 (5 months)
🔥 Integration Phase – GS + CA Deep Dive + PYQ Mastery
  • Community & Preventive Dentistry – 2 weeks (Epidemiology, national programs, indices, fluorides, biostatistics)
  • Public Health Dentistry – 2 weeks (Dental public health, health education, government schemes)
  • General Studies Deep Dive:
    • Indian Polity & Constitution – 2 weeks
    • Indian History (Modern) – 2 weeks
    • Geography (India + UP) – 1.5 weeks
    • Indian Economy basics – 1 week
    • Science & Technology – 1 week
    • UP Special – Culture, History, Economy, Geography, Government Schemes – 2 weeks
  • PYQ Marathon: Solve ALL available previous year papers (2015-2025) – analyze patterns
  • Current Affairs Consolidation: Monthly compilations, health sector updates, dental policy changes
📌 Parallel Task: Daily 50 MCQs (30 dental + 10 GS + 10 CA). Make error journal. Revise weak topics every weekend.
Phase 4: Jul 2026 – Oct 2026 (4 months)
🏆 Revision + Mock Test Phase – THE FINAL PUSH
  • Month 1 (July): Complete revision of ALL dental subjects using notes. 2 full mock tests per week.
  • Month 2 (August): Revision of GS + heavy CA focus (last 1 year compilation). Daily 100 MCQs. 3 mock tests per week.
  • Month 3 (September): Targeted revision of weak areas from mock test analysis. Daily 150 MCQs. Mock test every alternate day.
  • Month 4 (October 1-28): Light revision of notes. Focus on high-yield topics. Last 6 months CA rapid revision.
    • Week 1-2: Full-length mocks + analysis
    • Week 3: Quick-fire revision of all subjects
    • Last 3 days: Only read your notes. Relax. Sleep well.
🚨 Critical: Do NOT start new topics in this phase. Only revise what you've studied. Trust your preparation. In the last week, focus on relaxation and confidence building.

🧠 Current Affairs Strategy - Step by Step

Exactly how to do CA daily without getting overwhelmed

🌅
Morning Routine (30-45 min)
The daily CA input session
  1. Step 1 (5 min): Open The Hindu app → Scan ALL headlines → Read only relevant ones (health, science, polity, economy, UP)
  2. Step 2 (10 min): Read 1-2 editorials that seem exam-relevant
  3. Step 3 (10 min): Open Drishti IAS daily CA → Read all bullet points → Star ★ the health/dental/UP ones
  4. Step 4 (5 min): Check UP news in Jagran/Amar Ujala (only state section)
  5. Step 5 (5-10 min): Make SHORT notes using the format below
📝
Note-Making Format
The WHAT-WHY-EXAM template
For each important news item, note:

📌 Topic: [One-line heading]
📋 What: [Key facts in 2-3 bullet points]
❓ Why Important: [Exam relevance]
🏷️ Tag: [Health/Polity/Science/UP/Economy]
🔗 Related: [Any related scheme/topic]
📅 Date: [dd/mm/yyyy]

Example:
📌 India launches Ayushman Arogya Mandir 2.0
📋 Renamed HWCs, 1.5L centers operational, free dental care included
❓ Directly asked in UPPSC health papers, public health dentistry
🏷️ Health + UP Special
🔗 Related to NHM, Ayushman Bharat, AB-PMJAY
🔄
Spaced Revision System
How to actually remember CA long-term
The 1-3-7-21-45 Rule:

📍 Day 1: Read and make notes (morning)
📍 Day 1 (Night): Quick re-read of today's notes (5 min)
📍 Day 3: Revise Day 1 notes without looking first, then verify
📍 Day 7: Weekly revision of entire week's CA (Saturday morning)
📍 Day 21: Revise 3-week-old notes (combine with monthly compilation)
📍 Day 45: Quick scan of 6-week-old notes (should be in monthly comp by now)

This is the MOST IMPORTANT habit. Without revision, you'll forget 80% within a week. Use this tracker's CA Log to mark revision status.

✍️ PYQ Practice - Sample Questions

Based on UPPSC Dental Surgeon previous year patterns • Click options to check answers

Q1 - Oral SurgeryUPPSC Pattern
Which of the following is the most common nerve injured during removal of impacted mandibular third molar?
a) Mental nerve
b) Inferior alveolar nerve
c) Lingual nerve
d) Mylohyoid nerve
✅ Answer: c) Lingual nerve
The lingual nerve is the most commonly injured nerve during surgical removal of mandibular third molars due to its close proximity to the lingual plate of the mandible in the third molar region. The IAN is the second most commonly affected.
Q2 - Oral PathologyUPPSC Pattern
Which of the following is a true neoplasm of odontogenic origin?
a) Ameloblastoma
b) Dentigerous cyst
c) Odontogenic keratocyst
d) Radicular cyst
✅ Answer: a) Ameloblastoma
Ameloblastoma is a true benign neoplasm of odontogenic epithelium. Dentigerous cyst and radicular cyst are developmental and inflammatory cysts respectively. OKC was reclassified as Odontogenic Keratocyst (cyst) in WHO 2017 classification.
Q3 - ProsthodonticsUPPSC Pattern
The most accurate impression material for fixed partial denture is:
a) Alginate
b) Zinc oxide eugenol
c) Polyvinyl siloxane (Addition silicone)
d) Polysulfide
✅ Answer: c) Polyvinyl siloxane
Addition silicone (PVS) is the most accurate elastomeric impression material with dimensional stability, excellent detail reproduction, and no by-products during setting. It has the least permanent deformation among all elastomers.
Q4 - PharmacologyUPPSC Pattern
Which local anesthetic is CONTRAINDICATED in patients with liver disease?
a) Articaine
b) Lidocaine
c) Procaine
d) Benzocaine
✅ Answer: b) Lidocaine
Lidocaine is an amide LA metabolized in the liver. In severe liver disease, its metabolism is significantly impaired, leading to toxicity. Ester LAs (procaine) are metabolized by plasma cholinesterase and are safer in liver disease. Articaine is partially metabolized in plasma, making it a better alternative.
Q5 - Current Affairs / HealthUPPSC Pattern
The "Ayushman Bharat - Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana" provides health insurance coverage up to:
a) ₹1 lakh per family per year
b) ₹3 lakh per family per year
c) ₹5 lakh per family per year
d) ₹10 lakh per family per year
✅ Answer: c) ₹5 lakh per family per year
AB-PMJAY launched on Sept 23, 2018. Covers 10.74 crore poor families. Provides cashless/paperless access to healthcare at empanelled hospitals. Covers secondary & tertiary care hospitalization up to ₹5 lakh per family per year.
Q6 - UP SpecialUPPSC Pattern
Which of the following is the state animal of Uttar Pradesh?
a) Nilgai
b) Gangetic Dolphin
c) Barasingha (Swamp Deer)
d) Indian Rhinoceros
✅ Answer: c) Barasingha (Swamp Deer)
State symbols of UP: Animal - Barasingha, Bird - Sarus Crane, Tree - Ashoka, Flower - Palash (Butea monosperma/Flame of the Forest), Fish - Chital (Moh). These are frequently asked in UPPSC exams.
Q7 - Conservative DentistryUPPSC Pattern
The ideal irrigant used during root canal treatment is:
a) Sodium hypochlorite
b) Chlorhexidine
c) Normal saline
d) Hydrogen peroxide
✅ Answer: a) Sodium hypochlorite (NaOCl)
NaOCl is the gold standard root canal irrigant. It has antibacterial action, dissolves organic tissue (necrotic and vital), and is a lubricant. Commonly used concentrations: 0.5% - 5.25%. Chlorhexidine is an adjunct but cannot dissolve organic tissue.
Q8 - PeriodonticsUPPSC Pattern
Gracey curette #13-14 is specifically designed for:
a) Anterior teeth
b) Premolar buccal/lingual
c) Posterior mesial surfaces
d) Posterior distal surfaces
✅ Answer: c) Posterior mesial surfaces
Gracey curettes: 1-2 & 3-4 (Anterior), 5-6 (Anterior & Premolar), 7-8 & 9-10 (Posterior buccal/lingual), 11-12 (Posterior mesial - some classify), 13-14 (Posterior mesial surfaces). Key: They are area-specific and have one cutting edge.
